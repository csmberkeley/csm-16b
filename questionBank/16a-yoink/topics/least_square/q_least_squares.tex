\qns{Least squares for trajectory}

Say we want to model the trajectory of some ball we kick. We know that that the ball kick follows the trajectory form $\alpha x^{2}+ \beta y =5$. We have collected 4 unique data points of our ball's trajectory, to help us complete our model. Unfortunately, these data points are noisy, and may not lie perfectly on the ball's real trajectory.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $y$ \\
\hline
-0.9 & 1.2 \\
\hline
-0.4 & 2.5 \\
\hline
0.8 & 1.5 \\
\hline
1.1 & 0.5 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center} 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Is this system undetermined or overdetermined - why?
    \ans{
        This system is overdetermined because our matrix A has more rows than columns. Overdetermined systems generally yield more accurate models than underdetermined systems because overdetermined systems typically represent more data, which can help us create a more representative model.
    }
    \item Set up a least squares equation that can be solved to complete our equation.
    \ans{
        \begin{center}
            
        $(-0.9)^{2} \alpha+1.2 \beta=5$ \\

        $(-0.4)^{2} \alpha+2.5=5$ \\

        $(0.8)^{2} \alpha+1.5=5$ \\

        $(1.1)^{2} \alpha+0.5=5$ \\

        $A \vec{x}=b$ \\

        $A\left[\begin{array}{l}\alpha \\ \beta\end{array}\right]=b$ \\

        $\left[\begin{array}{cc}(-0.9)^{2} & 1.2 \\ (-0.4)^{2} & 2.5 \\ (0.8)^{2} & 1.5 \\ (1.1)^{2} & 0.5\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\alpha \\ \beta\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}5 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 5\end{array}\right]$ \\

        $\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.81 & 1.2 \\ 0.16 & 2.5 \\ 0.64 & 1.5 \\ 1.21 & 0.5\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\alpha \\ \beta\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}5 \\ 5 \\ 5 \\ 5\end{array}\right]$ 

        \end{center}
    }
    \item As learned in lecture, least squares attempts to minimize the error quantity that can be written as $\|\vec{b}-A \vec{x}\|$. For $\|\vec{b}-A \vec{x}\|$ to be minimized, $\vec{b}-A \vec{x}$ must be orthogonal to the column space of matrix A. Apply this idea to determine an expression for the unknown vector $\vec{x}$.
    \ans{
        \begin{center}
            
        Since we know $\vec{b}-A \vec{x}$ is orthogonal to the column space of matrix A, we can write: \\

        $<\vec{b}-A \vec{x}, \operatorname{col}(A)>=0$

        If $\vec{b}-A \vec{x}$ is orthogonal to the column space of $\mathrm{A}$ its orthogonal to each column of $\mathrm{A}$ ie.: \\

        $<\vec{b}-A \vec{x}, a_{i}>=0$ for every $\mathrm{i}$ \\

        $(\vec{b}-A \vec{x}) \cdot a_{i}^{T}=0$ \\

        Let $\vec{e}=(\vec{b}-A \vec{x})$ \\

        We can write

        $$
        e \cdot\left[\begin{array}{llll}
        a_{1}^{T} & a_{2}^{T} & \ldots & a_{i}^{T}
        \end{array}\right]=0
        $$

        We can replace the above expression with \\

        $e \cdot A^{T}=0$ \\

        $A^{T}(\vec{b}-A \vec{x})=0$ \\

        $A^{T} \vec{b}=A^{T} A \vec{x}$ \\

        $\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} \vec{b}=\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1}\left(A^{T} A\right) \vec{x}$ \\

        $\left(A^{T} A\right)^{-1} A^{T} \vec{b}=\vec{x}$ 
        \end{center}

    }
    \item Solve the equation to complete our model - feel free to use a calculator 
    \ans{
    \begin{center}

        $$\vec{x}=\left(\left[\begin{array}{cccc}
        0.81 & 0.16 & 0.64 & 1.21 \\
        1.2 & 2.5 & 1.5 & 0.5
        \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ll}
        0.81 & 1.2 \\
        0.16 & 2.5 \\
        0.64 & 1.5 \\
        1.21 & 0.5
        \end{array}\right]\right)^{-1}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}
        0.81 & 0.16 & 0.64 & 1.21 \\
        1.2 & 2.5 & 1.5 & 0.5
        \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}
        5 \\
        5 \\
        5 \\
        5
        \end{array}\right]
        $$

        Calculator yields:

        $$
        \vec{x}=\left[\begin{array}{l}
        3.444 \\
        1.804
        \end{array}\right]
        $$
    \end{center}

    }
\end{enumerate}
